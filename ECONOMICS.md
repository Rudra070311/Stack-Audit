# What Is a Converted Lead Worth?

Assuming Credex earns through margins on discounted infrastructure credits, a converted startup customer could realistically be worth somewhere between $2,000 and $15,000 annually depending on company size and AI usage.

A reasonable midpoint estimate:

* average startup AI spend managed through Credex: $20,000/year,
* estimated effective margin or retained value: ~15%.

That makes a converted customer worth roughly:

* $3,000 ARR to Credex.

This is obviously approximate, but it creates a useful working model.

---

## Estimated CAC by Channel

### Reddit + Hacker News Organic

Cost:

* essentially $0 cash,
* but high founder time investment.

Estimated CAC:

* $50–150 equivalent founder time per qualified lead.

---

### Founder Outreach on X

Manual outbound to founders discussing AI costs.

Estimated CAC:

* ~$100–250 equivalent time cost per converted consultation.

This channel scales poorly but works early.

---

### Community-Based Distribution

Slack groups, Discords, founder communities.

Estimated CAC:

* probably lowest overall,
* around $30–100 equivalent effort cost.

Trust transfers faster in niche communities.

---

## Funnel Economics

Reasonable early funnel assumptions:

100 completed audits

↓

25 lead captures (25%)

↓

6 consultation calls booked (24%)

↓

1 converted paying customer (~16%)

If one converted customer is worth ~$3,000 ARR, then:

* every 100 completed audits potentially generates ~$3,000 ARR.

At small scale, this is already profitable because acquisition costs are mostly founder time rather than paid advertising.

The model breaks if:

* consultation booking rates fall below ~10%,
* or if most audits reveal tiny savings that are not actionable.

---

## What Needs To Be True To Reach $1M ARR In 18 Months

Using the rough estimate:

* $3,000 ARR per converted customer.

To reach $1M ARR:

* ~333 active customers are needed.

Assuming:

* 1% conversion from completed audits to paying customers,

the platform would need:

* roughly 33,000 completed audits over 18 months.

That equals:

* ~1,830 completed audits per month,
* or ~60/day.

That is ambitious but not impossible if:

* the audit outputs become highly shareable,
* benchmark data improves,
* and Credex develops strong reputation inside startup AI infrastructure circles.

The more realistic challenge is not traffic. It is maintaining trust and recommendation quality at scale. If the recommendations feel biased or inaccurate, conversion rates collapse quickly.

The business works only if the audits remain genuinely useful even before a sale happens.
